Gameplay

Pig is a dice game invented in 1945. To play, take turns rolling a single die as many times as you want. Each time you roll, add the result to your running total. However, if you roll a 1, you lose all your points for that round!

Example: Alyssa starts her turn and rolls a 4. Alyssa could hold and only score 4 points, but she's a daredevil, so she rolls again. Then, she rolls a 6 taking her turn point total to 10. She is on fire, so she decides to roll again. She rolls a 1, and must end her turn without scoring. The computer rolls a sequence 2-5-3-4-3 for a turn total of 17, and adds 17 to its running score.

Two Dice Pig

A variation of Pig where you use two dice instead of one. Roll both dice at the same time, if neither shows a 1, add the sum of the dice to your turn total. If a 1 is rolled on either dice, you score nothing and end your turn. If two 1's are rolled, your running total returns to 0 along with your turn total. If you roll any other doubles, you must roll again.

Big Pig

A variation of Pig with the same rules as Two-Dice Pig except doubles are great! If you roll two 1's, you get to add 25 points to your score. If you roll any other doubles, add twice the value of the dice to your overall point total.

Skunk

Skunk is Pig adapted for large groups. Instead of rolling the dice, players start by standing up. At any point in the round a player can sit reserving their points for that round, and adding that number to their overall point total. Skunk is played in five rounds, one for each letter of the word 'SKUNK'. After five rounds, the person with the most points is the winner.
